Artist statement

What began as learning to sketch has slowly become a kind of handwriting — a way to be seen, especially by myself, especially when the world wouldn’t.

I tried every medium I could get my hands on, copying still subjects in front of me. Over time the work moved toward something faster and more honest: pages made on trains and in cafés, figures drawn from memory, and eventually portraits pulled straight from imagination — moods, grief, the quiet debates I have with myself. These days I mostly draw myself. I like myself enough to be my own muse.

The journey

Five chapters · 2021 — now
Chapter 01

First, every medium

When I started, I tried everything — watercolour, markers, pastel, gouache, pen. I quickly knew I didn’t love landscapes; I loved faces. I could only draw what sat still in front of me, but I was hungry to work in every material at once.

Beginnings
Chapter 02

Drawing in real time

Then I got quicker, and a little braver — live, restless subjects caught in the moment. I’d discovered mixed-media artists online and stopped sticking to one medium: pastel over gouache, pen into acrylic. It felt like my sketches had come to life.

Real time
Chapter 03

Sketches from the road

Travel pages — of people, places and things. Each one holds the memory of where I was so strongly that revisiting it takes me right back, every single time.

On the road
Chapter 04

Learning the figure

An online course taught me to build the human face and body from structure. Game-changing: I no longer needed the subject in front of me. I could draw from mind within a framework — freer, less of a watch-and-draw artist.

The figure
